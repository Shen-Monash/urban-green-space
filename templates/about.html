Green Space

“The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in

2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into

the future. At its heart are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call

for action by all countries - developed and developing - in a global partnership. They recognize that

ending poverty and other deprivations must go hand-in-hand with strategies that improve health and

education, reduce inequality, and spur economic growth - all while tackling climate change and

working to preserve our oceans and forests.”

- United Nations General Assembly (United Nations, 2022)

 

In these 17 goals, the 11th goal focuses on “Sustainable Cities and Communities” (United Nations,

2022), and the seventh target of this goal is to “provide universal access to safe, inclusive and

accessible, green and public spaces, in particular for women and children, older persons and

persons with disabilities” (United Nations, 2022).

 

Future Vision

On this site we aim to explore how everybody can contribute to making Urbanised environments greener.

While it may be in the hands of governments and city councils to make parks, playgrounds, and plant trees

and plants around the community, everyone can find a way to contribute to the green spaces they have

access to. Whether you live in a small apartment, a clustered unit block, or even a small home, everyone

should have access to the knowledge that allows them to make their own space greener. The benefits are

numerous, including physical health benefits, mental health benefits, and social benefits.
